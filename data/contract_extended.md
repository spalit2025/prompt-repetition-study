## SECTION 5. SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT

5.1 **Availability.** For any managed analytics platform or hosted solution provided pursuant to a Statement of Work, the Provider shall maintain a minimum system availability of ninety-nine and one-half percent (99.5%) during each calendar month, measured as the total number of minutes in the applicable month minus the number of minutes of Unscheduled Downtime, divided by the total number of minutes in the applicable month (the "Uptime Commitment").

5.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.** The Provider shall perform routine maintenance during the maintenance window of Saturday 02:00 AM to 06:00 AM Eastern Time. The Provider shall provide the Client with at least seventy-two (72) hours' prior written notice of any scheduled maintenance that is expected to result in service unavailability exceeding thirty (30) minutes.

5.3 **Service Credits.** In the event that the Provider fails to meet the Uptime Commitment in any given calendar month, the Client shall be entitled to service credits as follows: (a) for availability between 99.0% and 99.49%, a credit equal to five percent (5%) of the monthly fees attributable to the affected service; (b) for availability between 98.0% and 98.99%, a credit equal to ten percent (10%) of such monthly fees; and (c) for availability below 98.0%, a credit equal to twenty percent (20%) of such monthly fees. Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall constitute the Client's sole and exclusive remedy for the Provider's failure to meet the Uptime Commitment, except as otherwise set forth in Section 8 pursuant to Section 4.2(b)(iii) of any applicable SOW.

5.4 **Incident Response.** The Provider shall maintain a dedicated incident response team and shall acknowledge all Critical Severity incidents (defined as incidents resulting in complete loss of service functionality affecting more than twenty-five percent (25%) of end users) within thirty (30) minutes of detection and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to restore service within four (4) hours.

5.5 **Service Level Governance.** The Provider shall designate a Technical Account Manager who shall serve as the primary point of contact for all matters relating to service availability, incident escalation, and compliance with the Uptime Commitment set forth in Section 5.1. The Technical Account Manager shall prepare and deliver monthly SLA performance reports to the Client within ten (10) business days following the end of each calendar month, and shall coordinate quarterly service review meetings with the Client's Authorized Representative. ## DPA SECTION 1. DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

1.3 **"Processing"** (and its cognates "Process," "Processed," and "Processes") means any operation or set of operations performed upon Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination, alignment, combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

1.4 **"Security Incident"** means any accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data transmitted, stored, or otherwise Processed by the Data Processor or any of its Approved Sub-Processors in connection with the Services.

1.5 **"Standard Contractual Clauses"** or **"SCCs"** means the standard contractual clauses for the transfer of personal data to processors established in third countries, as approved by the European Commission pursuant to Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914 of June 4, 2021, as amended or replaced from time to time.

1.6 **"Approved Sub-Processor"** means a third-party entity authorized by the Client to Process Personal Data on behalf of the Provider in connection with the Services, as listed in Schedule 2 of this DPA.

1.7 **"Data Protection Impact Assessment"** or **"DPIA"** means an assessment carried out in accordance with Article 35 of the GDPR or equivalent provisions under other Applicable Data Protection Laws, to evaluate the necessity, proportionality, and risks of Processing activities with respect to the rights and freedoms of Data Subjects.

## DPA SECTION 7. TECHNICAL AND ORGANIZATIONAL SECURITY MEASURES

7.1 **General Obligation.** The Provider shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk of Processing, taking into account the state of the art, the costs of implementation, the nature, scope, context, and purposes of Processing, and the risk of varying likelihood and severity for the rights and freedoms of Data Subjects. These measures shall meet or exceed the requirements set forth in this DPA Section 7 and shall be designed to protect Personal Data against unauthorized or unlawful Processing and against accidental loss, destruction, or damage.

7.2 **Encryption Standards.** The Provider shall implement the following encryption requirements: (a) all Personal Data at rest shall be encrypted using Advanced Encryption Standard with 256-bit key length (AES-256) or a cryptographic algorithm of equivalent or greater strength; (b) all Personal Data in transit shall be encrypted using Transport Layer Security version 1.3 (TLS 1.3) or higher; and (c) all encryption keys shall be managed in accordance with industry-standard key management practices, including secure generation, storage, rotation (at minimum every twelve (12) months), and destruction.

7.3 **Access Controls.** The Provider shall implement and maintain: (a) role-based access controls ensuring that only authorized personnel with a legitimate business need may access Personal Data; (b) multi-factor authentication for all remote access to systems containing Personal Data; (c) unique user identification and authentication credentials for all personnel with access to Personal Data; and (d) automatic session termination after fifteen (15) minutes of inactivity for systems containing Personal Data.

7.4 **Certification and Audit Requirements.** The Provider shall: (a) maintain a current Service Organization Control 2 (SOC 2) Type II certification, issued by an independent third-party auditor, covering the trust service criteria of security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy; (b) provide the Client with a copy of its most recent SOC 2 Type II report within thirty (30) days of the Client's written request, but no more than once per calendar year; and (c) promptly notify the Client of any material findings, qualifications, or exceptions identified in any SOC 2 Type II report that relate to the Processing of Personal Data under this DPA.

CLAIMS AGAINST THE DPA LIABILITY CAP SHALL NOT REDUCE THE AMOUNTS AVAILABLE UNDER THE CAPS SET FORTH IN SECTION 8 OF THE AGREEMENT, AND VICE VERSA.

11.3 **Exclusions.** THE DPA LIABILITY CAP SHALL NOT APPLY TO: (A) LIABILITIES ARISING FROM THE PROVIDER'S WILLFUL MISCONDUCT OR GROSS NEGLIGENCE IN THE PROCESSING OF PERSONAL DATA; (B) FINES OR PENALTIES IMPOSED BY A SUPERVISORY AUTHORITY DIRECTLY AGAINST THE PROVIDER; OR (C) THE PROVIDER'S INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS UNDER SECTION 9 OF THE AGREEMENT TO THE EXTENT SUCH OBLIGATIONS ARISE FROM A VIOLATION OF THIS DPA.
