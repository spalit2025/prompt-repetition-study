## SECTION 1. DEFINITIONS

1.1 **"Affiliate"** means, with respect to any Party, any entity that directly or indirectly controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with such Party, where "control" means the ownership of more than fifty percent (50%) of the voting securities or equivalent ownership interest.

1.2 **"Authorized Representative"** means the individual designated by each Party to serve as the primary point of contact for matters arising under this Agreement, as set forth in Section 10.4 of this Agreement.

1.3 **"Confidential Information"** means all non-public information disclosed by one Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other Party (the "Receiving Party"), whether orally, in writing, or in any other form, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, trade secrets, business plans, financial data, customer lists, technical specifications, algorithms, source code, and any information derived therefrom.

1.4 **"Deliverables"** means all work product, reports, analyses, models, software, documentation, and other materials developed by the Provider in the course of performing the Services under this Agreement or any Statement of Work.

1.5 **"Intellectual Property Rights"** means all patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, and any other proprietary rights recognized under applicable law.

1.6 **"Personal Data"** has the meaning ascribed to it under the applicable data protection laws, including but not limited to the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and the New York SHIELD Act, and shall include any information that identifies, relates to, describes, is reasonably capable of being associated with, or could reasonably be linked, directly or indirectly, with a particular individual or household.

1.7 **"Services"** means the data analytics, consulting, machine learning model development, and related professional services to be performed by the Provider as described in this Agreement and in any Statement of Work executed by the Parties.

1.8 **"Statement of Work"** or **"SOW"** means a written document executed by both Parties that describes specific Services to be performed, Deliverables to be provided, timelines, fees, and other terms applicable to a particular engagement under this Agreement.

## SECTION 2. SCOPE OF SERVICES

2.1 **Engagement.** The Provider shall perform the Services described in each Statement of Work in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards and practices for similar services. Each SOW shall be sequentially numbered (e.g., SOW-001, SOW-002) and shall be deemed incorporated into and governed by the terms of this Agreement.

2.2 **Statements of Work.** Each Statement of Work shall specify, at a minimum: (a) a detailed description of the Services to be performed; (b) the Deliverables to be provided; (c) the project timeline and milestones; (d) the fees and payment schedule; and (e) any specific assumptions, dependencies, or acceptance criteria.

2.3 **Change Orders.** Either Party may request modifications to an existing SOW by submitting a written change order. No change order shall be effective unless signed by the Authorized Representatives of both Parties.

2.4 **Provider Personnel.** The Provider shall assign qualified personnel to perform the Services and shall not replace any key personnel identified in a SOW without prior written notice to the Client and the Client's reasonable approval.

---

## SECTION 3. TERM AND RENEWAL

3.1 **Initial Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for an initial term of three (3) years, unless earlier terminated in accordance with Section 11 of this Agreement (the "Initial Term").

3.2 **Renewal.** Upon expiration of the Initial Term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive periods of one (1) year each (each a "Renewal Term"), unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal to the other Party not less than ninety (90) days prior to the expiration of the then-current term. The Initial Term and any Renewal Term(s) shall collectively be referred to as the "Term."

3.3 **Survival.** The provisions of Sections 1, 6, 7, 8, 9, 13, and 14 shall survive the expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason.

## SECTION 4. COMPENSATION AND PAYMENT TERMS

4.1 **Fees.** The Client shall pay the Provider the fees specified in each applicable Statement of Work for the Services performed thereunder. Unless otherwise specified in the applicable SOW, the Provider's standard hourly rates shall be as follows: (a) Senior Data Scientist: Three Hundred Seventy-Five Dollars ($375.00) per hour; (b) Data Engineer: Two Hundred Ninety-Five Dollars ($295.00) per hour; (c) Project Manager: Two Hundred Fifty Dollars ($250.00) per hour; and (d) Junior Analyst: One Hundred Seventy-Five Dollars ($175.00) per hour.

4.2 **Invoicing and Payment.**
(a) The Provider shall submit monthly invoices in arrears, detailing Services performed, hours expended by personnel category, and applicable fees.
(b) The Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within forty-five (45) days of receipt of a properly submitted invoice ("Net 45"). Payment shall be made by wire transfer to the Provider's designated account.
(c) The Client shall notify the Provider in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt of any disputed invoice. The Parties shall resolve any dispute in good faith within thirty (30) days.

4.3 **Late Payments.** Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at the rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is less, calculated from the date such payment was due until the date of actual payment. In addition, if any undisputed payment is more than thirty (30) days overdue, the Provider may, upon fifteen (15) days' prior written notice to the Client, suspend performance of the Services until all overdue amounts, including accrued interest, have been paid in full.

4.4 **Expenses.** The Client shall reimburse the Provider for all reasonable, pre-approved, out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including but not limited to travel, lodging, and third-party software licensing costs. The Provider shall submit expense reports with supporting documentation within thirty (30) days of incurring such expenses.

4.5 **Taxes.** All fees and other amounts payable under this Agreement are exclusive of all applicable sales, use, value-added, withholding, and other taxes. The Client shall be responsible for all such taxes, except for taxes based on the Provider's income.

4.6 **Expense Reimbursement Cap.** Notwithstanding Section 4.4, aggregate reimbursable expenses incurred by the Provider in any twelve (12) month period under an applicable Statement of Work shall not exceed fifteen percent (15%) of the total annual fees payable under such SOW (the "Expense Cap"). Any expenses exceeding the Expense Cap shall require the prior written approval of the Client's Authorized Representative designated pursuant to Section 10.4, and the Provider shall not be entitled to reimbursement for any amounts in excess of the Expense Cap absent such approval. For the avoidance of doubt, the Expense Cap shall be calculated on a per-SOW basis and shall not be aggregated across multiple Statements of Work.

## SECTION 5. SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT

5.1 **Availability.** For any managed analytics platform or hosted solution provided pursuant to a Statement of Work, the Provider shall maintain a minimum system availability of ninety-nine and one-half percent (99.5%) during each calendar month, measured as the total number of minutes in the applicable month minus the number of minutes of Unscheduled Downtime, divided by the total number of minutes in the applicable month (the "Uptime Commitment").

5.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.** The Provider shall perform routine maintenance during the maintenance window of Saturday 02:00 AM to 06:00 AM Eastern Time. The Provider shall provide the Client with at least seventy-two (72) hours' prior written notice of any scheduled maintenance that is expected to result in service unavailability exceeding thirty (30) minutes.

5.3 **Service Credits.** In the event that the Provider fails to meet the Uptime Commitment in any given calendar month, the Client shall be entitled to service credits as follows: (a) for availability between 99.0% and 99.49%, a credit equal to five percent (5%) of the monthly fees attributable to the affected service; (b) for availability between 98.0% and 98.99%, a credit equal to ten percent (10%) of such monthly fees; and (c) for availability below 98.0%, a credit equal to twenty percent (20%) of such monthly fees. Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall constitute the Client's sole and exclusive remedy for the Provider's failure to meet the Uptime Commitment, except as otherwise set forth in Section 8 pursuant to Section 4.2(b)(iii) of any applicable SOW.

5.4 **Incident Response.** The Provider shall maintain a dedicated incident response team and shall acknowledge all Critical Severity incidents (defined as incidents resulting in complete loss of service functionality affecting more than twenty-five percent (25%) of end users) within thirty (30) minutes of detection and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to restore service within four (4) hours.

5.5 **Service Level Governance.** The Provider shall designate a Technical Account Manager who shall serve as the primary point of contact for all matters relating to service availability, incident escalation, and compliance with the Uptime Commitment set forth in Section 5.1. The Technical Account Manager shall prepare and deliver monthly SLA performance reports to the Client within ten (10) business days following the end of each calendar month, and shall coordinate quarterly service review meetings with the Client's Authorized Representative. ## SECTION 6. CONFIDENTIALITY

6.1 **Obligations.** The Receiving Party agrees to: (a) hold the Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party in strict confidence; (b) not disclose such Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party, except as permitted under Section 6.2; (c) use such Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement; and (d) protect such Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care as it uses to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

6.2 **Permitted Disclosures.** The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its employees, agents, and contractors who have a need to know, provided such persons are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those herein. The Receiving Party may also disclose Confidential Information as required by applicable law, regulation, or court order, provided prompt written notice is given to the Disclosing Party (to the extent legally permitted).

6.3 **Exceptions.** Confidential Information shall not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party; (b) was rightfully in the Receiving Party's possession prior to disclosure; (c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information; or (d) is rightfully obtained from a third party without restriction.

6.4 **Return of Materials.** Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party and certify such return or destruction in writing within thirty (30) days.

## SECTION 8. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

8.1 **Exclusion of Consequential Damages.** EXCEPT FOR BREACHES OF SECTION 6 (CONFIDENTIALITY), SECTION 10 (DATA PROTECTION), OR INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS UNDER SECTION 9, NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOSS OF PROFITS, LOSS OF DATA, OR BUSINESS INTERRUPTION, ARISING OUT OF THIS AGREEMENT, REGARDLESS OF LEGAL THEORY, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

8.2 **General Cap on Liability.** EXCEPT FOR A PARTY'S BREACH OF SECTION 6 (CONFIDENTIALITY) OR SECTION 10 (DATA PROTECTION), THE AGGREGATE LIABILITY OF EITHER PARTY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED TWO MILLION FIVE HUNDRED THOUSAND DOLLARS ($2,500,000.00) (THE "GENERAL LIABILITY CAP").

8.3 **Enhanced Cap for Data Breaches.** NOTWITHSTANDING SECTION 8.2, IN THE EVENT OF A BREACH OF SECTION 10 (DATA PROTECTION) THAT RESULTS IN THE UNAUTHORIZED ACCESS, DISCLOSURE, OR LOSS OF PERSONAL DATA, THE AGGREGATE LIABILITY OF THE RESPONSIBLE PARTY SHALL NOT EXCEED FIVE MILLION DOLLARS ($5,000,000.00) (THE "DATA BREACH LIABILITY CAP").

8.4 **Per-Incident Cap.** IN ADDITION TO THE FOREGOING CAPS, THE LIABILITY OF EITHER PARTY FOR ANY SINGLE INCIDENT OR SERIES OF RELATED INCIDENTS ARISING FROM THE SAME ROOT CAUSE SHALL NOT EXCEED FIVE HUNDRED THOUSAND DOLLARS ($500,000.00) PER INCIDENT, SUBJECT TO THE APPLICABLE AGGREGATE CAP SET FORTH IN SECTION 8.2 OR 8.3, AS APPLICABLE.

8.5 **Applicability.** The limitations set forth in this Section 8 shall apply to the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, regardless of whether either Party has been advised of the possibility of such damages, and regardless of the failure of essential purpose of any limited remedy.

## SECTION 11. TERMINATION

11.1 **Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement for any reason or no reason by providing ninety (90) days' prior written notice to the other Party (the "Termination Notice Period").

11.2 **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice to the other Party if: (a) the other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail; (b) the other Party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy protection, or has a receiver or trustee appointed for a substantial portion of its assets; or (c) the other Party ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

11.3 **Effect of Termination.** Upon termination: (a) the Client shall pay for all Services performed through the effective date of termination; (b) each Party shall return or destroy Confidential Information per Section 6.4; (c) the Provider shall deliver all Deliverables for which the Client has paid; and (d) all licenses granted hereunder shall terminate, except as set forth in Section 3.3.

11.4 **Transition Assistance.** Upon request, the Provider shall provide transition assistance for up to sixty (60) days following termination, at the Provider's then-current standard hourly rates.

## SECTION 13. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

13.1 **Negotiation.** In the event of any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement, or the breach, termination, or invalidity thereof (a "Dispute"), the Parties shall first attempt to resolve such Dispute through good faith negotiation between the Authorized Representatives designated in Section 10.4. Such negotiation shall commence within ten (10) business days of one Party's written notice to the other Party of the Dispute.

13.2 **Mediation.** If the Parties are unable to resolve the Dispute through negotiation within thirty (30) days of the commencement of such negotiation, the Parties shall submit the Dispute to mediation administered by JAMS in accordance with its then-current mediation rules. The mediation shall take place in New York, New York. The costs of the mediator shall be shared equally by the Parties.

13.3 **Arbitration.** If the Dispute is not resolved through mediation within sixty (60) days, the Dispute shall be finally resolved by binding arbitration administered by JAMS in New York, New York, under the JAMS Comprehensive Arbitration Rules then in effect. The arbitration shall be conducted by three (3) arbitrators. The arbitral award shall be final, binding, and enforceable in any court of competent jurisdiction.

13.4 **Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of New York, without giving effect to any choice of law or conflict of law provisions that would cause the application of the laws of any other jurisdiction.

13.5 **Equitable Relief.** Notwithstanding the foregoing, either Party may seek injunctive or other equitable relief from any court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm pending the resolution of a Dispute through the procedures set forth in this Section 13.
